Siem Reap

Gateway to the ancient temples of Angkor and a charming destination in its own right

The Temple Gateway

Siem Reap, meaning "Defeat of Siam" (now Thailand), is a provincial capital and the gateway to the world-famous Angkor temple complex. While most visitors come to see the ancient temples, Siem Reap itself is a charming destination with a rich history, beautiful French colonial architecture, and a vibrant cultural scene.

The town has transformed from a sleepy provincial capital into a bustling tourist hub while maintaining its authentic Cambodian character. The Old Market area and Pub Street offer a lively atmosphere with restaurants, bars, and shops, while the surrounding countryside provides opportunities to experience traditional Cambodian village life.

Pub Street & Nightlife

Pub Street is the heart of Siem Reap's nightlife and dining scene. During the day, it's relatively quiet, but come evening, it transforms into a vibrant hub of activity. The street is lined with restaurants serving both Khmer and international cuisine, bars, and shops.

While Pub Street is touristy, it offers a fun atmosphere and is a great place to meet other travelers. For a more authentic experience, explore the side streets and alleys where you'll find local restaurants and hidden gems.

Beyond the Temples

Siem Reap offers much more than just temple visits. Take a boat trip on Tonle Sap Lake, Southeast Asia's largest freshwater lake, to see floating villages and experience local life on the water. Visit the Angkor National Museum to learn about the history and art of the Khmer Empire.

The town is also home to several excellent markets, including the Old Market (Phsar Chas) and the Night Market, where you can shop for souvenirs, handicrafts, and local products. For a cultural experience, attend a traditional Apsara dance performance, which showcases classical Khmer dance.

Visitor Tips

Stay Duration

Plan to spend at least 3-4 days in Siem Reap to fully explore the Angkor temples and the town itself. The temple complex is vast, and rushing through it means missing many incredible sites.

Temple Tours

Hire a tuk-tuk driver for the day to visit the temples - they know the best routes and timing. Start early to avoid crowds and heat. Consider hiring a guide for deeper historical insights.

Weather

The dry season (November to April) is the best time to visit. Bring plenty of water, sunscreen, and a hat when exploring temples. The heat can be intense, especially at midday.

Budget Tips

Siem Reap offers accommodation for all budgets. Eat at local restaurants away from Pub Street for authentic food at lower prices. Many guesthouses offer free bicycle rentals.
