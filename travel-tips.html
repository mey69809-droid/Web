Travel Tips

Essential information to help you plan your perfect Cambodian adventure

Best Time to Visit

Dry Season (November - April)

The best time to visit Cambodia is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. This period offers pleasant weather with lower humidity and minimal rainfall.

Highlights: Cool temperatures (especially December-February), clear skies, perfect for temple visits and beach activities.

Note: December and January are peak tourist months, so expect higher prices and more crowds.

Wet Season (May - October)

The wet season brings daily afternoon showers, high humidity, and lush green landscapes. While less popular, this season has its advantages.

Highlights: Fewer tourists, lower prices, lush vegetation, and the spectacular sight of Tonle Sap Lake expanding.

Note: Rain usually falls in short bursts, so it won't ruin your entire day. Bring an umbrella and plan indoor activities for afternoons.

Special Events

Consider timing your visit with major festivals. Khmer New Year (mid-April) and Water Festival (November) are spectacular but also very busy.

Khmer New Year: Three-day celebration with traditional games and ceremonies.

Water Festival: Boat races on the Tonle Sap River in Phnom Penh.

Transportation

Getting to Cambodia

Cambodia has three international airports: Phnom Penh (PNH), Siem Reap (REP), and Sihanoukville (KOS). Major airlines connect Cambodia to regional hubs like Bangkok, Singapore, and Ho Chi Minh City.

You can also enter by land from Thailand, Vietnam, or Laos. Popular border crossings include Poipet (Thailand), Bavet (Vietnam), and Stung Treng (Laos).

Getting Around

Buses: Comfortable and affordable, connecting major cities. Popular companies include Giant Ibis and Mekong Express.

Tuk-tuks: The most common form of local transport. Always negotiate the price before getting in.

Motorbikes: Can be rented in major cities, but only if you're experienced. Traffic can be chaotic.

Safety Information

General Safety

Cambodia is generally a safe country for tourists. However, as with any destination, take common-sense precautions:

  • Keep valuables secure and out of sight
  • Be cautious at night, especially in isolated areas
  • Use reputable transportation services
  • Keep copies of important documents
  • Be aware of your surroundings in crowded areas
Health Precautions

Before traveling to Cambodia:

  • Consult your doctor about vaccinations (Hepatitis A & B, Typhoid, Tetanus recommended)
  • Consider malaria prophylaxis for rural areas
  • Drink only bottled or filtered water
  • Be cautious with street food (though it's generally safe)
  • Pack a basic first-aid kit

Currency & Money

Currency

The official currency is the Cambodian Riel (KHR), but US Dollars are widely accepted and often preferred. Most prices are quoted in USD, especially for hotels, tours, and restaurants.

Exchange Rate: Approximately 4,000 Riel = 1 USD (check current rates before traveling).

Tips: Always carry small denominations. Riel is typically used for small purchases and change. Make sure USD bills are in good condition - torn or old bills may be rejected.

Payment Methods

Cash: Cash is king in Cambodia. Most transactions are done in cash, especially in smaller establishments and markets.

ATMs: Widely available in cities and tourist areas. They dispense both USD and Riel. Check with your bank about international fees.

Credit Cards: Accepted at upscale hotels, restaurants, and some shops, but not everywhere. Always carry cash as backup.

Additional Tips

Visa Requirements

Most nationalities can obtain a visa on arrival at airports and land borders. Tourist visas cost $30 USD and are valid for 30 days. Bring a passport photo and cash for the visa fee.

You can also apply for an e-visa online before traveling, which may save time at the border.

Language

Khmer is the official language, but English is widely spoken in tourist areas. Learning a few basic Khmer phrases (hello: "sous-dey", thank you: "aw-koon") is appreciated by locals.

Tipping

Tipping is appreciated but not always expected. A 10% tip at restaurants is standard. For tuk-tuk drivers and guides, small tips are appreciated for good service.
