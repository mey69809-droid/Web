About Cambodia

Discover the Kingdom of Wonder - a land of ancient temples, rich culture, and warm hospitality

History

Cambodia has a rich and complex history spanning over two millennia, from the powerful Khmer Empire to modern-day recovery and growth.

The Khmer Empire (802-1431)

The Khmer Empire was one of the most powerful empires in Southeast Asia, ruling over much of the region from its capital at Angkor. During this golden age, the Khmer built magnificent temples including Angkor Wat, Bayon, and hundreds of other structures that still stand today.

The empire was known for its sophisticated irrigation systems, impressive architecture, and cultural achievements. At its peak, Angkor was one of the largest pre-industrial cities in the world.

Colonial Period (1863-1953)

Cambodia became a French protectorate in 1863, which lasted until 1953. The French colonial period left a lasting architectural legacy, particularly in Phnom Penh, and introduced Western education and administrative systems.

Modern Cambodia

After gaining independence in 1953, Cambodia experienced periods of peace and conflict. The country has worked hard to rebuild and develop since the 1990s, with tourism playing an increasingly important role in the economy.

Geography

Location & Size

Cambodia is located in Southeast Asia, bordered by Thailand to the west and northwest, Laos to the north, Vietnam to the east, and the Gulf of Thailand to the southwest.

The country covers approximately 181,035 square kilometers (69,898 square miles), making it slightly smaller than the state of Oklahoma in the United States.

Major Features

Mekong River: One of the world's great rivers, flowing through Cambodia and providing water for agriculture and transportation.

Tonle Sap Lake: Southeast Asia's largest freshwater lake, which expands dramatically during the wet season, creating a unique ecosystem.

Cardamom Mountains: A mountain range in the southwest, home to diverse wildlife and protected areas.

Tourism Significance

A Growing Tourism Destination

Tourism is one of Cambodia's most important industries, contributing significantly to the country's economy and providing employment for hundreds of thousands of people.

The country's main attractions include:

  • Angkor Archaeological Park: A UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the world's most important archaeological sites
  • Cultural Heritage: Rich traditions, festivals, and arts including Apsara dance
  • Natural Beauty: Pristine beaches, tropical islands, and diverse ecosystems
  • Authentic Experiences: Opportunities to experience traditional Cambodian life and hospitality

Cambodia offers excellent value for travelers, with affordable accommodations, food, and activities, making it accessible to a wide range of budgets.

Warm Hospitality

Cambodians are known for their friendly and welcoming nature. Visitors often comment on the genuine warmth and hospitality they experience throughout the country.

Ancient Wonders

The temples of Angkor represent one of humanity's greatest architectural achievements and continue to inspire awe in visitors from around the world.

Resilient Spirit

Despite a difficult recent history, Cambodia has shown remarkable resilience and is building a bright future while preserving its rich cultural heritage.
