Food & Culture

Experience the rich flavors and traditions of Cambodian cuisine and culture

Traditional Cambodian Cuisine

Amok

Amok is perhaps Cambodia's most famous dish - a creamy, aromatic curry typically made with fish, coconut milk, and a blend of traditional spices. The dish is traditionally steamed in banana leaves, which gives it a unique flavor and presentation.

The curry paste includes ingredients like lemongrass, galangal, turmeric, and kaffir lime leaves, creating a complex and fragrant flavor profile. Amok can be found in restaurants throughout Cambodia and is a must-try for visitors.

Lok Lak

Lok Lak is a popular Cambodian stir-fry dish made with marinated beef (or sometimes chicken or pork) served with a tangy dipping sauce. The meat is typically marinated in soy sauce, oyster sauce, and black pepper, then quickly stir-fried.

The dish is usually served with a fried egg on top, fresh vegetables, and a dipping sauce made from lime juice, black pepper, and salt. It's a hearty and flavorful dish that's popular throughout Cambodia.

Fresh Spring Rolls

Cambodian fresh spring rolls (nime chow) are light, healthy, and refreshing. Unlike fried spring rolls, these are made with rice paper wrappers filled with fresh vegetables, herbs, and sometimes shrimp or pork.

They're typically served with a sweet and tangy dipping sauce and are perfect for a light meal or snack. The combination of fresh herbs, crisp vegetables, and the chewy rice paper creates a delightful texture and flavor.

Fish Amok

The classic version of amok made with fresh river fish, steamed to perfection in banana leaves.

Khmer Curry

A milder curry compared to Thai curries, made with coconut milk, lemongrass, and local spices.

Cambodian Culture

Apsara Dance

The classical Khmer dance form, Apsara, is a traditional dance that tells stories through graceful movements and elaborate costumes. This ancient art form dates back to the Angkor period and is considered a symbol of Cambodian culture.

Performances can be seen at cultural shows in Siem Reap and Phnom Penh, often accompanied by traditional music played on instruments like the roneat (xylophone) and tro (fiddle).

Buddhism & Temples

Buddhism is the dominant religion in Cambodia, with over 95% of the population practicing Theravada Buddhism. Temples (wats) are central to Cambodian life, serving as places of worship, education, and community gathering.

Visitors should dress modestly when visiting temples, covering shoulders and knees. It's customary to remove shoes before entering temple buildings and to show respect to monks and religious images.

Festivals & Celebrations

Cambodia celebrates numerous festivals throughout the year. The Water Festival (Bon Om Touk) in November is one of the largest, featuring boat races on the Tonle Sap River. Khmer New Year in April is another major celebration with traditional games and ceremonies.

Traditional Crafts

Cambodia is known for its traditional handicrafts including silk weaving, silverwork, stone carving, and pottery. Artisan workshops can be visited in Siem Reap and Phnom Penh, where you can see these crafts being made and purchase authentic souvenirs.
