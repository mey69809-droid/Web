Phnom Penh

The vibrant capital city where ancient traditions meet modern Cambodia

The Charming Capital

Phnom Penh, the capital and largest city of Cambodia, is a fascinating blend of old and new. Situated at the confluence of the Mekong, Tonle Sap, and Bassac rivers, this city has been the national capital since French colonization of Cambodia and has grown to become the nation's economic, industrial, and cultural center.

Despite its rapid modernization, Phnom Penh retains much of its traditional charm. The city is home to beautiful French colonial architecture, bustling markets, serene Buddhist temples, and the magnificent Royal Palace. The city's name means "Hill of Penh" and refers to the legend of Lady Penh who discovered sacred objects on the hill where Wat Phnom now stands.

Royal Palace & Silver Pagoda

The Royal Palace of Phnom Penh is a complex of buildings that serves as the royal residence of the King of Cambodia. Built in 1866, the palace is a fine example of Khmer architecture with a French touch. The complex includes the Throne Hall, the Moonlight Pavilion, and the Silver Pagoda.

The Silver Pagoda, also known as Wat Preah Keo, houses many national treasures including a life-sized gold Buddha decorated with 9,584 diamonds. The floor is covered with more than 5,000 silver tiles, each weighing 1 kilogram, giving the pagoda its name.

Markets & Local Life

Phnom Penh is famous for its vibrant markets. The Central Market (Phsar Thmei) is housed in a beautiful Art Deco building and offers everything from jewelry to electronics. The Russian Market (Phsar Toul Tom Poung) is popular with tourists for its handicrafts, silk, and souvenirs.

The city's riverside promenade is a popular spot for locals and tourists alike, especially at sunset. Here you can enjoy street food, watch traditional Khmer dance performances, or simply relax and watch the boats on the river.

Visitor Tips

Getting Around

Tuk-tuks are the most popular way to get around the city. Always negotiate the price before getting in. For longer distances, consider using ride-sharing apps or renting a motorbike if you're experienced.

Shopping

Visit the markets early in the morning for the best selection and prices. Don't forget to bargain - it's expected! The Russian Market is great for souvenirs, while Central Market offers a wide variety of goods.

Cultural Sites

When visiting the Royal Palace and temples, dress modestly (cover shoulders and knees). Remove your shoes before entering temple buildings and be respectful of worshippers.

Food & Dining

Try the street food along the riverside - it's safe and delicious! Don't miss traditional dishes like Amok (curry), Lok Lak (stir-fried beef), and fresh spring rolls. The city has excellent restaurants for all budgets.
